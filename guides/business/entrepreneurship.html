Entrepreneurship and Business Finance

Starting a Business

  • Identify a Problem: The best businesses solve real problems
  • Validate Your Idea: Test before building fully
  • Know Your Market: Understand your target customers
  • Start Small: MVP approach - minimum viable product

Business Structures

  • Sole Proprietorship: Simplest, but personal liability
  • LLC: Limited liability, pass-through taxation
  • S-Corp: Tax advantages for profitable businesses
  • C-Corp: For raising venture capital

Funding Options

  • Bootstrapping: Self-fund from savings or revenue
  • Friends & Family: Early funding from your network
  • Small Business Loans: SBA loans, bank loans
  • Angel Investors: Individual investors for early stage
  • Venture Capital: Institutional funding for high-growth
  • Crowdfunding: Kickstarter, Indiegogo

Business Finance Basics

  • Revenue: Money from sales
  • Expenses: Costs of running the business
  • Profit: Revenue minus expenses
  • Cash Flow: Money in vs money out timing
  • Profit Margin: Profit as percentage of revenue

Financial Management

  • Separate business and personal finances
  • Track every expense from day one
  • Set aside money for taxes (30%+)
  • Build a business emergency fund
  • Use accounting software (QuickBooks, Wave)

Key Metrics to Track

  • Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
  • Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
  • Lifetime value (LTV)
  • Burn rate
  • Runway
