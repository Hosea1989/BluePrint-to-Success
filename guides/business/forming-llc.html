What is an LLC?

A Limited Liability Company (LLC) is a business structure that provides personal liability protection while offering flexibility in taxation and management.

Benefits of an LLC

  • Limited Liability: Personal assets protected from business debts
  • Pass-Through Taxation: Profits taxed once on your personal return
  • Flexibility: Less paperwork than corporations
  • Credibility: Appears more professional than sole proprietorship

Steps to Form an LLC

  1. Choose a Name: Must be unique in your state, typically ends in "LLC"
  2. Choose a Registered Agent: Person to receive legal documents
  3. File Articles of Organization: Submit to Secretary of State
  4. Create Operating Agreement: Outlines ownership and operations
  5. Get EIN: Employer Identification Number from IRS (free)
  6. Open Business Bank Account: Keep finances separate
  7. Get Licenses/Permits: Check local requirements

Costs

  • Filing fees: $50-$500 depending on state
  • Annual fees: Some states charge ongoing fees
  • Registered agent: $0-$300/year

Best States for LLCs

  • Wyoming: Low fees, strong privacy
  • Delaware: Business-friendly courts
  • Your Home State: Often simplest option

After Formation

  • Open a business bank account
  • Get business insurance if needed
  • Set up accounting system
  • Understand your tax obligations
