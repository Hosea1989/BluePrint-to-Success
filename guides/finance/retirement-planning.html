Retirement Planning

Introduction

Planning for retirement is one of the most important financial decisions you'll make. Start early to take advantage of compound growth.

Retirement Accounts

  • 401(k): Employer-sponsored, often with matching contributions. Contribute at least enough to get the full match!
  • Traditional IRA: Tax-deductible contributions, taxed on withdrawal. Good if you expect lower taxes in retirement.
  • Roth IRA: After-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals. Great for young earners expecting higher future income.
  • SEP IRA: For self-employed individuals. Higher contribution limits.

How Much to Save

  • General Rule: Save 10-15% of income for retirement
  • The 4% Rule: You can withdraw 4% of your retirement savings annually
  • Target: Aim for 25x your annual expenses saved by retirement

Investment Strategy by Age

  • 20s-30s: Aggressive growth (80-90% stocks)
  • 40s-50s: Balanced approach (60-70% stocks)
  • 60s+: Conservative (40-50% stocks, more bonds)

Key Actions

  1. Start saving as early as possible
  2. Maximize employer 401(k) match
  3. Open a Roth IRA if eligible
  4. Increase contributions with each raise
  5. Review and rebalance annually
