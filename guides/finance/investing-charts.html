Introduction

Understanding how to read financial charts is essential for making informed investment decisions. This guide covers basic chart reading techniques and investment concepts.

Types of Charts

  • Line Charts: Simple view of price movement over time
  • Bar Charts: Show open, high, low, and close prices
  • Candlestick Charts: Visual representation of price action with colored bodies

Key Chart Patterns

  • Support and Resistance: Price levels where buying or selling pressure emerges
  • Trends: Uptrend (higher highs), Downtrend (lower lows), Sideways
  • Moving Averages: Smooth out price data to identify trends

Technical Indicators

  • RSI (Relative Strength Index): Measures momentum, overbought/oversold conditions
  • MACD: Trend-following momentum indicator
  • Volume: Confirms price movements

Fundamental vs Technical Analysis

  • Fundamental: Analyzes company financials, earnings, and value
  • Technical: Studies price patterns and market psychology
  • Best Approach: Use both for comprehensive analysis
