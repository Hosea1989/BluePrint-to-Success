Introduction

Behavioral finance studies how psychology affects financial decisions. Understanding your biases helps you make better choices.

Common Biases

  • Loss Aversion: Losses feel twice as painful as gains feel good. Can lead to holding losing investments too long.
  • Confirmation Bias: Seeking information that confirms what you already believe.
  • Herd Mentality: Following the crowd, buying high and selling low.
  • Overconfidence: Thinking you can beat the market consistently.
  • Anchoring: Fixating on a specific price point (like what you paid).
  • Recency Bias: Overweighting recent events in decision-making.

Emotional Traps

  • FOMO: Fear of missing out drives buying at peaks
  • Panic Selling: Fear during downturns locks in losses
  • Euphoria: Excessive optimism leads to ignoring risks

Strategies to Combat Bias

  • Create an investment plan and stick to it
  • Automate investments to remove emotion
  • Diversify to reduce single-stock attachment
  • Review decisions with a cooling-off period
  • Keep a decision journal to learn from mistakes
