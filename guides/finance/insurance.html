Introduction

Insurance protects you from financial losses. Understanding different types helps you make informed coverage decisions.

Types of Insurance

  • Health Insurance: Covers medical expenses. Essential for protecting against catastrophic costs.
  • Auto Insurance: Required in most states. Covers accidents, theft, and liability.
  • Renters/Homeowners Insurance: Protects your belongings and provides liability coverage.
  • Life Insurance: Provides for dependents if you pass away. Term is usually better than whole life.
  • Disability Insurance: Replaces income if you can't work due to illness or injury.

Key Terms

  • Premium: What you pay for coverage (monthly or annually)
  • Deductible: What you pay before insurance kicks in
  • Coverage Limit: Maximum amount the insurer will pay
  • Copay: Fixed amount you pay for a service

Tips for Choosing Insurance

  • Higher deductible = lower premium (but more out-of-pocket risk)
  • Shop around and compare quotes
  • Bundle policies for discounts
  • Review coverage annually
