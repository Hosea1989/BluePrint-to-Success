Debt Management

Introduction

Managing debt effectively is essential for financial health and achieving long-term goals. This guide provides strategies for reducing and eliminating debt.

Types of Debt

  • Good Debt: Investments in your future, like student loans and mortgages. These typically have lower interest rates and can build assets or increase earning potential.
  • Bad Debt: High-interest debt, such as credit card balances. This type of debt costs you money without building value.

Debt Repayment Strategies

Snowball Method

Pay off your smallest debts first while making minimum payments on larger debts. As each small debt is paid off, roll that payment into the next smallest debt.

  • Pros: Quick wins build momentum and motivation
  • Cons: May pay more interest over time

Avalanche Method

Focus on paying off debts with the highest interest rates first, regardless of balance size.

  • Pros: Saves the most money on interest
  • Cons: May take longer to see progress

Consolidation and Refinancing

  • Debt Consolidation: Combine multiple debts into one loan with a lower interest rate. Simplifies payments and can reduce total interest.
  • Refinancing: Replace an existing loan with a new one at a lower interest rate. Common for mortgages and student loans.
  • Balance Transfer: Move high-interest credit card debt to a card with a 0% introductory APR.

Tips for Getting Out of Debt

  1. Stop adding new debt - cut up credit cards if needed
  2. Create a realistic budget that prioritizes debt payments
  3. Find ways to increase income (side hustles, overtime)
  4. Negotiate with creditors for lower interest rates
  5. Celebrate milestones to stay motivated

When to Seek Help

  • Credit Counseling: Non-profit agencies can help create a debt management plan
  • Debt Settlement: Negotiate to pay less than you owe (impacts credit score)
  • Bankruptcy: Last resort option for overwhelming debt
