Introduction

Understanding taxes is crucial for financial planning and minimizing tax liability. This guide covers the basics of income tax, deductions, and credits.

Understanding Taxes

  • Income Tax: Tax on earnings from work (wages, salaries) and investments (dividends, capital gains).
  • Marginal Tax Rate: The tax rate on your last dollar of income. The US uses progressive tax brackets.
  • Effective Tax Rate: Your total tax paid divided by total income. Usually lower than your marginal rate.

Deductions vs Credits

  • Deductions: Reduce your taxable income. A $1,000 deduction saves you $220 if you're in the 22% bracket.
  • Credits: Reduce your actual tax bill dollar-for-dollar. A $1,000 credit saves you $1,000.
  • Standard Deduction: A flat amount you can deduct without itemizing ($13,850 single, $27,700 married in 2023).
  • Itemized Deductions: Specific deductions like mortgage interest, state taxes, and charitable donations.

Common Deductions

  • Mortgage interest
  • State and local taxes (SALT) - capped at $10,000
  • Charitable contributions
  • Medical expenses (above 7.5% of AGI)
  • Student loan interest (up to $2,500)
  • Self-employment expenses

Common Credits

  • Child Tax Credit
  • Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
  • Education credits (American Opportunity, Lifetime Learning)
  • Retirement savings credit (Saver's Credit)
  • Energy efficiency credits

Tax Planning Strategies

  • Maximize Retirement Contributions: 401(k) and IRA contributions reduce taxable income.
  • HSA Contributions: Triple tax advantage for healthcare expenses.
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: Sell losing investments to offset gains.
  • Timing Income and Deductions: Shift income or deductions between years strategically.

Filing Taxes

  • Tax Software: TurboTax, H&R Block, FreeTaxUSA for DIY filing.
  • Tax Professional: CPA or tax preparer for complex situations.
  • Free File: IRS Free File for income under $73,000.
  • Deadline: April 15th (or next business day).
